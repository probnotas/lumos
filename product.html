Lumos Architect

Non-invasive dream interface. Featherweight design.

Lumos reads your sleep architecture in real time, guiding you into lucid states with gentle neural feedback.

So you can explore, create, heal and rest in ways never before possible.

The era of conscious sleep is here.

Take a closer look.

Crafted from aerospace-grade matte-black polymer and lined with medical-grade silicone, the Architect is built to sit comfortably against skin for eight hours straight — no pressure points, no irritation.

Five precision LED indicators on the front module display your current sleep stage — light, deep, or REM — and double as a battery gauge during charging, so you always know the headband's status at a glance.

The rear strap features ventilation slots for airflow and a micro-adjust buckle system flanked by cushioned pads, ensuring a secure, breathable fit for every head size through the night.

Technology

Explore what's inside Architect.

Detection

Reading your brain. In real time.

Architect houses four medical-grade EEG channels embedded seamlessly into the headband's inner lining. These dry-electrode sensors continuously monitor cortical activity across the frontal and temporal lobes — detecting the precise moment your brain transitions from light sleep into deep slow-wave stages and, ultimately, into REM. The system classifies sleep phases with clinical-level accuracy in under three seconds, giving Architect the intelligence to act at exactly the right moment.

Action

The signal that changes everything.

Once REM is confirmed, Architect activates its bone-conduction transducers — delivering a precisely calibrated audio cue lasting just 50 to 100 milliseconds. The sound is imperceptible to anyone nearby, but your sleeping brain registers it clearly. Over successive nights, this cue becomes neurally linked to an intention you set before bed — a problem to solve, a skill to rehearse, a place to revisit. The result: a reliable neural pathway between external stimulus and conscious awareness inside the dream, dramatically increasing your probability of lucidity.

Three layers. One outcome.

What happens when you sleep with Architect.

Layer 1

Drift.

Before you dream, you need to rest. Lumos continuously monitors your sleep cycles — detecting micro-arousals, optimizing REM transitions, and nudging your brain toward deeper, more restorative stages. Most users report measurably better sleep within the first week.

Layer 2

Imprint.

Lumos uses bone conduction to trigger Targeted Memory Reactivation during sleep. Subtle audio cues replayed at precisely the right sleep stage allow your brain to consolidate, strengthen, and review information — turning eight hours of rest into eight hours of learning.

Layer 3

Nightwork.

Set an intention before you sleep — a problem to solve, a skill to rehearse, a place to visit. Lumos detects the onset of REM and delivers precisely timed neural cues that bring you to full consciousness inside the dream, letting you act with purpose while your body rests.
